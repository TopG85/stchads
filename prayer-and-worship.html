Prayer and Worship

The worshipping and prayer life of any church is its lifeblood. Our main service on a Sunday morning has grown in the past year with new folk from all over the world joining us for gathered worship. In addition to our Sunday service we have a Wednesday morning service at 10.30am using the BCP which is warmly appreciated by those who attend.

During 2024/2025 we upgraded our sound and vision system which has had a very positive effect for those who attend in person and for those who watch online.

We have also begun to celebrate Holy Communion in the way that it was traditionally celebrated before the pandemic. People are invited to come to the communion rail where they are offered the bread and wine at the front. The wine is now offered either from a shared cup or, for hygiene reasons, from an individual cup. The communicant has the choice at the rail which one to accept. The option is also offered for non-alcoholic wine.

Similarly our prayer meetings have started to meet in person again. Once a month we gather on the first Tuesday in the evening to pray together and this is well attended and appreciated.

For the past few years we've been blessed with big numbers of asylum seekers attending our services. Again it was a privilege to take some of them through an Alpha course this year and to baptise a number of them at the end. The local hotel was closed by the government at Christmas so the majority of men have been moved elsewhere.

Whilst the hotel has closed and the asylum seekers have moved from the area we are being joined by new people from other parts of the world, particularly Africa, who are moving into the area for work purposes and have begun attending our fellowship. This has resulted in a truly international congregation on Sunday mornings, and is a joy!
